# Monitoring and Support Ecosystem for Manuel and Laura

## Manuel's Functionalities

### 1. Smart Coffee Maker with Pill Dispenser
The main solution for Manuel is a coffee maker equipped with technology that integrates a medication dispensing system.

- When the coffee maker button is pressed:
  - Two blisters are deployed, each containing a pill.

- Each blister includes an LED indicator system:
  - 🔶 Orange light: indicates the pill has not yet been taken.
  - 🟢 Green light: indicates the pill has been taken.

- User flow:
  1. Manuel starts the coffee maker.
  2. The two blisters appear.
  3. When a blister is opened, the LED turns orange.
  4. If Manuel takes the pill and closes the blister:
     - The LED automatically changes to green.

- Dose replacement:
  - There is an additional button next to the blisters.
  - This button dispenses a new pill **only if two conditions are met**:
    1. The blister is open.
    2. The LED is orange.
  - This allows replacing a pill if it is lost or dropped.

---

### 2. Home Entry and Exit Sensor
Manuel has a sensor installed on his front door.

- Functionality:
  - Detects when Manuel leaves and enters his home.
  - Calculates how long he stays outside.

- Example:
  - If he leaves at 2:00 PM and returns at 2:30 PM:
    - The system records a 30-minute outing.

- All this data is automatically sent to Laura’s application.

---

### 3. Gamification System (Fridge Display)
Manuel interacts with a screen placed on his fridge that displays missions.

- Types of missions:
  1. **Favors** requested by Laura:
     - Example: “Dad, can you go buy chicken for this week?”
  
  2. **Challenges with rewards**:
     - Example: going out for 40 minutes.

- Rewards:
  - If he completes certain challenges, he receives rewards such as:
    - A fruit salad delivered to his home.
    - Other food-based incentives.

- Objective:
  - Motivate Manuel to stay active and engaged with his well-being.

---

## Laura’s Functionalities (Mobile Application)

### 1. Monitoring Manuel’s Habits
Laura’s app receives all the data collected from the ecosystem.

- Monitored data:
  - Whether Manuel took his medication.
  - What time he took it.
  - Whether he left the house.
  - Time of departure and duration.

---

### 2. Mission Creation
From the app, Laura can create personalized missions.

- These missions are sent directly to Manuel’s fridge display.
- They can be:
  - Favors.
  - Reward-based challenges.

---

### 3. Reward System
Laura defines rewards associated with challenges.

- Example:
  - If Manuel goes out for 40 minutes:
    - Laura can send him a fruit salad delivery.

- Objective:
  - Encourage healthy habits and maintain emotional connection between them.

---

## Ecosystem Summary
The system connects physical devices (coffee maker, door sensor, fridge display) with a mobile application, enabling:

- Real-time monitoring.
- Gamification-based incentives.
- Remote support from Laura.

This creates a comprehensive experience of care, motivation, and companionship for Manuel.
